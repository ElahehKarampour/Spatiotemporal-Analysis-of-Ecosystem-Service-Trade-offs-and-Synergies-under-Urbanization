This repository presents a comprehensive geospatial analysis framework for investigating the spatiotemporal dynamics of ecosystem services (ESs) and their interactions under rapid urban expansion. The study focuses on the San Antonio metropolitan region, Texas, USA, and evaluates how urban growth between 2000 and 2024 has influenced the relationships among key ecosystem services.
The project integrates remote sensing, GIS, spatial statistics, and ecological modeling to identify ecosystem service synergies and trade-offs, detect ecosystem service bundles, quantify urbanization intensity, and reveal significant local spatial associations using Bivariate Local Moran’s I (LISA).
